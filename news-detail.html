Criminal Defense

The first 48 hours after arrest.

Home / News / Detail
Criminal Jul 12, 2026 By Hiromi Higuruma 6 min read

The first hours after an arrest often set the direction of the entire case. Not because trial has started — but because statements, evidence handling, and access to counsel are forming here.

The right to remain silent is not a formality. Many clients feel they must “explain” to look cooperative, when explanations without counsel present can become the foundation of charges that are hard to unwind later.

Contact counsel before any formal interview. In our practice, an early request for an attorney often stops disproportionate pressure and ensures every answered question is actually necessary.

Document the timeline privately: who detained you, at what time, what was asked, and who was present. Those notes are not official evidence, but they help the team build motions, procedural objections, and defense strategy.

If family or colleagues are contacted, point them to the firm's emergency line. One clean channel of communication is worth more than ten informal tips in a police waiting room.
